How the score is calculated

Each sector is assigned a weight tier reflecting its approximate share of total market capitalization: Heavy = 3, Medium = 2, Light = 1. The seven cyclical sectors and four defensive sectors each get a weighted average of their weekly % change, so heavier sectors move the basket average more than lighter ones.

Spread = Cyclical weighted avg − Defensive weighted avg

Score = 5.5 + (Spread × 0.75), clamped between 1 and 10

5.5 is the midpoint of the 1–10 scale, so a spread of zero (cyclicals and defensives moved by the same amount) lands exactly on neutral. The 0.75 multiplier sets how sensitive the gauge is — a roughly 3-point spread between the two baskets in a single week is enough to reach the extreme zones.

Bands: 1–3 Extreme risk-off · 3–4.75 Risk-off · 4.75–6.25 Neutral · 6.25–8 Risk-on · 8–10 Extreme risk-on

This is an educational, illustrative tool built on simplified weights and a single-week snapshot. It is not a validated quantitative model and the weight tiers are approximations, not live index data. Nothing here is financial advice — use it as one input among many, not a signal to act on by itself.
